Q1. What are life processes?

Life processes are basic activities like nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion that living organisms perform to stay alive.

Q2. What is nutrition? Name its types.

Nutrition is the process of taking in and using food. It is of two types: Autotrophic (in plants) and Heterotrophic (in animals).

Q3. Why is respiration important?

Respiration helps in breaking down food to release energy which is necessary for all life activities.

Q4. What is the difference between aerobic and anaerobic respiration?

Aerobic respiration occurs in the presence of oxygen, while anaerobic respiration takes place without oxygen.

Q5. What is transportation in human beings?

Transportation is the movement of substances like oxygen, nutrients, and waste products in the body through the circulatory system.

Q6. Describe the structure and function of the human heart.

The heart is a muscular organ with four chambers. It pumps blood throughout the body and maintains circulation.

Q7. What is excretion and why is it necessary?

Excretion is the removal of waste products from the body. It is necessary to maintain a healthy internal environment.
